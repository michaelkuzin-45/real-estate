    <h1>The Surname Kuzin (Кузин)</h1>

    <div class="origin-box">
        <h3>Quick Summary</h3>
        <p><strong>Origin:</strong> Russian / Slavic<br>
        <strong>Meaning:</strong> "Son of Kuzma" (from the Greek <em>Kosmos</em>)<br>
        <strong>Historical Root:</strong> Order, Decency, and the Universe.</p>
    </div>

    <h2>1. Etymological Roots</h2>
    <p>The name is a patronymic derived from the Greek name <strong>Kuzma</strong>. It gained popularity in Eastern Europe through <strong>St. Cosmas</strong>, a physician and martyr known for his selfless healing.</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Kuzya:</strong> The common diminutive or nickname.</li>
        <li><strong>Kuzin:</strong> Meaning "belonging to Kuzya."</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>2. Noble Connections</h2>
    <p>While the name is widespread among various social classes, a branch of the Kuzin family was officially recognized in the <strong>General Armorial of the Noble Families of the Russian Empire</strong>.</p>

    <p>According to Imperial records, the noble Kuzin crest typically features symbols of military merit:</p>
